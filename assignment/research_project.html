Examining Adverse Birth Outcomes in Santa Clara County

Due by 11:59 PM on Thursday, January 30, 2025

I. Introduction

A. Research Topic and Importance

  • Overview of adverse birth outcomes (e.g., preterm birth, low birth weight) in Santa Clara County.
  • Importance of social and environmental determinants (tree equity, area deprivation, air pollution, housing, gentrification).
  • Introduction to available data: Santa Clara County Preterm Births GIS dataset (source).

B. Selecting a Target Journal

  • Potential Journals:
    • Environmental Health Perspectives (focus on environmental determinants of health)
    • American Journal of Public Health (broader public health & equity focus)
    • Maternal and Child Health Journal (specific to birth outcomes)
  • Formatting Considerations:
    • Follow journal word limits, citation styles (APA, AMA, etc.), and structured sections (Abstract, Methods, Results, Discussion).
    • Use LaTeX (Overleaf) or RMarkdown for manuscript formatting.

C. Research Question(s)

  1. Primary Question:
    • What environmental and social determinants (tree equity, area deprivation, air pollution, housing instability, gentrification) are associated with preterm birth rates in Santa Clara County?
  2. Sub-questions:
    • How do neighborhood-level socioeconomic factors (housing affordability, displacement, area deprivation) impact preterm birth risk?
    • What is the relationship between gentrification and birth outcomes?
    • Do areas with higher tree equity scores and lower pollution levels have lower rates of adverse birth outcomes?

II. Theoretical Framework: Organized Abandonment Theory

A. Definition of Organized Abandonment

  • Organized Abandonment (Gilmore, 2007) refers to state-sanctioned neglect of marginalized communities, particularly through disinvestment, racialized urban policies, and structural inequalities.
  • Check out this paper on the association between organized abandonment and policing published in Social Science & Medicine.

B. Application to Preterm Birth Outcomes

  • Housing instability and gentrification: Rising rents, eviction, and displacement lead to stress, instability, and poorer maternal health outcomes.
  • Environmental hazards: Industrial zoning, lack of tree cover, and proximity to highways contribute to higher air pollution and exposure to toxins, disproportionately affecting low-income communities of color.
  • Healthcare access disparities: Disinvestment in public health infrastructure reduces prenatal care access, exacerbating preterm birth risks.

IV. Research Methods

A. Unit of Analysis

  • Census tracts in Santa Clara County (spatial unit of analysis).
  • Outcome variable: Percentage of preterm births per tract.
  • Independent variables:
    • Housing & Gentrification: Median Rent, Rent-Burdened Households, Displacement Index
    • Environmental Variables: Tree Equity Score, Air Pollution (PM2.5, ozone exposure)
    • Socioeconomic Indicators: Area Deprivation Index (ADI)

VII. Expected Outcomes & Policy Implications

A. Anticipated Findings

  • Areas with high ADI scores and rising gentrification may have higher preterm birth rates.
  • Areas with greater tree cover and lower air pollution may have better birth outcomes.

B. Policy Implications

  • Identify high-risk areas for public health interventions.
  • Advocate for affordable housing policies to reduce displacement.
  • Promote urban greening and environmental justice initiatives.

VIII. Conclusion & Next Steps
